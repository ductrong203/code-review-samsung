<note>**[Performance]** The set intersection operation `max_tokens_per_item.keys() & mm_counts.keys()` creates a new set object in every call. While small, this is inside a hot path for model profiling/initialization.

**[Code Defect]** The intersection of keys (`max_tokens_per_item.keys() & mm_counts.keys()`) is used to calculate total tokens. If a modality exists in `mm_counts` but is missing from `max_tokens_per_item` (or vice versa), those tokens are silently ignored, leading to an underestimation of the total token count and potential OOM/crash during inference.

**[Maintainability and Readability]** The logic for calculating total_mm_tokens and performing the warning check is becoming complex and tightly coupled with the profiling loop. This logic should be encapsulated within a helper method or the `BaseProcessingInfo` class to improve readability and maintainability.

The set intersection `max_tokens_per_item.keys() & mm_counts.keys()` is clever but potentially confusing for maintainers. It obscures the intent of calculating the dot product of available counts and max tokens.</note>
