**[Code Defect]** The parameter 'num' is passed as a size_t but cast to int32_t in lv_anim_set_values. If 'num' exceeds the range of int32_t, this will cause an integer overflow/truncation bug.

The use of `(int32_t)num` in `lv_anim_set_values` assumes that `size_t num` will always fit within an `int32_t`. If `num` is very large, this could lead to truncation or unexpected behavior.

If 'num' is provided by an untrusted source and is excessively large, it could lead to integer overflow or out-of-bounds access when the animation index is later used to index the 'dsc' array in index_change().</note>
