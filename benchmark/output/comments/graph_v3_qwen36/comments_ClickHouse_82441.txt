If `n` is large but not large enough to trigger the overflow check (e.g., due to rounding or specific values), the multiplication could still overflow. The check should be `if (n != 0 && n > std::numeric_limits<std::size_t>::max() / element_size)` to handle the case where `n` is 0, and ensure that the multiplication does not overflow.
